Sales Revenue Recognition

In 2021, the main products of the Group's wires and cables business unit include bare copper wires, wires and 
cables. The fluctuation in prices of bare copper wires is often subject to the movement in prices of raw materials, 
and thus some of the sales prices are set according to the market prices agreed under the contracts at the time of 
shipments. The Group prepares reports on point of sale transactions by referring to the actual shipments and 
market price adjustments as the basis for revenue recognition. Due to the large number of transactions and 
different market prices that have been agreed upon by customers, the processing, recording and maintenance of 
such reports are performed manually in which their amounts are significant to the consolidated financial 
statements. Therefore, the accuracy of revenue recognized from sales of bare copper wires was considered as a 
key audit matter. Refer to Notes 4 and 24 to the consolidated financial statements for related accounting policies 
and disclosure of information relating to revenue recognition.

Our audit procedures performed in respect of the above key audit matter were as follows:

1. We obtained an understanding and tested the reasonableness of revenue recognition policy and internal 
control procedures over the sales of bare copper wires, and evaluated the effectiveness of relevant internal 
controls.

2. We performed sampling and reconciliation of sales prices and quantities with their respective amounts in 
the contracts and verified the accuracy of market price adjustments.

3. We verified the accuracy of monthly reports by recalculating the sales revenue and confirmed that the 
recognized amounts were consistent with those recorded in the general ledger.
